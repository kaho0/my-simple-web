About islam

Islam (/ˈɪzlɑːm/;[8] Arabic: ٱلْإِسْلَام‎, romanized: al-Islām, lit.  'submission [to the will of God]') is an Abrahamic monotheistic religion centered on the Quran and the teachings of Muhammad, the religion's founder. Adherents of Islam are called Muslims, who are estimated to number approximately 1.9 billion worldwide and are the world's second-largest religious population after Christians.
